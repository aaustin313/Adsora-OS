Subscriber Segment Analysis

Video Ads (iOS) vs. Lead Magnet Ads — Which segment deserves more budget?

Adsora  |  March 2026  |  Data: Beehiiv Cohort & CTR Analysis (Apr 2025 – Feb 2026)

1 Executive Summary

This report compares two subscriber acquisition segments to determine optimal budget allocation:

  • Video Ads (iOS targeting) — $3.50 per subscriber, 100% iOS audience
  • Lead Magnet Ads (mixed targeting) — $1.50 per subscriber, ~50/50 iOS/Android split

We analyzed cohort retention (% active over time) and verified click-through rates (CTR). Because CTR is calculated as a percentage of openers (not total subscribers), the correct formula for actual clicks per subscriber accounts for both open rate and CTR.

Bottom Line: Video ad subscribers have ~1.9x the verified CTR of lead magnet subscribers (0.65% vs 0.35%). When properly accounting for the CTR-on-openers math, both segments produce a nearly identical cost per click (~$2.94/click/year). However, video subscribers generate ~2.3x the clicks per subscriber per year (1.19 vs 0.51), making them significantly more valuable on a per-subscriber basis for a daily-send, click-dependent business. The recommendation is to invest primarily in video ads.

2 Apple Mail Privacy Protection — Industry Research

Before analyzing our data, it's critical to understand how Apple Mail Privacy Protection (MPP) distorts open rate metrics. Here is what the industry research confirms:

2.1 — What MPP Does

Introduced in September 2021, Apple's Mail Privacy Protection pre-loads all email content — including tracking pixels — through Apple's proxy servers, regardless of whether the subscriber actually opens the email. This means every iOS Apple Mail user with MPP enabled (estimated at 97%+ of all Apple Mail users) registers as an "open" automatically.

2.3 — What This Means for Our Data

Our video ad segment is 100% iOS. This means virtually all open-rate-based metrics ("% active") are unreliable — the raw ~50% retention at Month 6+ is substantially inflated by phantom opens. The lead magnet segment (~50% iOS) is also partially inflated, but less severely.

Critically: Click-through rates are not affected by MPP. Clicks require human action and cannot be faked. However, CTR is calculated as a percentage of openers — so when the open denominator is inflated by MPP, the reported CTR appears lower than the true engagement rate of real openers.

Note on Beehiiv: Beehiiv does filter some MPP noise from its analytics, so the "% active" numbers shown are not raw inflated figures. However, the filtering is imperfect, and iOS-heavy audiences will still show inflated activity rates.

6 Corrected Click Economics

6.1 — Why the Math Matters

CTR is calculated as a percentage of openers, not total subscribers. To calculate actual clicks per subscriber, we must account for open rate:

Clicks per subscriber per email = Open Rate × CTR

This is important because a subscriber who never opens will never click, regardless of the CTR among openers.

7 Analysis

7.1 — The iOS Inflation Problem (Research-Backed)

Industry data confirms that Apple MPP inflates open rates dramatically. Omeda's 6-month study showed total open rates nearly doubling from 22.6% to 40.5% after MPP launched. Apple Mail users can show near-100% open rates because MPP pre-loads every email automatically. With 97%+ of Apple users having MPP enabled, our 100%-iOS video segment's "% active" data is significantly overstated.

However, this inflation also affects the CTR calculation: because the open denominator is inflated by phantom opens, the reported CTR for iOS audiences appears lower than it actually is among real openers. This means the video segment's true CTR among genuinely engaged openers is likely even higher than the reported 0.65%.

7.2 — Equal Cost Per Click, Unequal Value Per Subscriber

With corrected math (CTR on openers, not total subscribers), both segments arrive at roughly the same cost per click per year (~$2.94). This might suggest it doesn't matter which segment you invest in. But that conclusion is wrong, because:

  • Video subscribers produce 2.3x more clicks per person per year. Each video subscriber is a significantly more productive asset sitting on your list. You're sending 365 emails/year — a subscriber who clicks 1.19 times is generating more than double the monetization opportunities of one who clicks 0.51 times.
  • More clicks per sub = more revenue touchpoints. If each click has a chance of converting into revenue (ad clicks, affiliate offers, product purchases), a subscriber generating 2.3x the clicks generates 2.3x the revenue opportunities over their lifetime.
  • Higher-clicking subscribers improve list-level deliverability. ISPs use engagement signals (including clicks) to determine inbox placement. A list with more active clickers gets better deliverability for everyone, amplifying the value across your entire subscriber base.
  • Volume matters for scale. To get the same total number of clicks from lead magnet subscribers, you'd need 2.3x more subscribers — which means managing a larger list, paying higher ESP costs, and dealing with more dead weight.

7.3 — The MPP Caveat on Our Math

The open rates we're using in our calculation are Beehiiv-filtered, but still partially inflated for the iOS segment. If the video segment's real open rate is lower than 50% (say 35-40% after fully stripping MPP inflation), then:

  • Clicks per sub per year would drop to ~0.83–0.95 (still higher than lead magnet's 0.51)
  • Cost per click per year would rise to ~$3.68–$4.22
  • Lead magnet would win on pure cost-per-click efficiency

However, the same logic applies: even with further deflated open rates, video subscribers still generate more clicks per person, making each subscriber a more valuable list asset.

8 Conclusions & Recommendations

Primary Recommendation: Video ad subscribers are the higher-quality segment. At equal cost-per-click economics and 2.3x the clicks per subscriber, they are the more productive and valuable audience. Allocate the majority of budget to video ads.

Rationale

  • 2.3x more clicks per subscriber per year — each video sub generates 1.19 clicks vs. 0.51 for lead magnet across 365 daily emails.
  • Cost per click is equal (~$2.94) — you're not paying more per click, you're getting more clicks per person.
  • Smaller, more engaged list is better than a larger, less engaged one — lower ESP costs, better deliverability, higher revenue per subscriber.
  • Video CTR is genuinely 1.9x higher — confirmed by verified clicks that cannot be inflated by Apple MPP.

Key Risks & Watch Items

  • Video CPL scalability: Monitor whether the $3.50 CPL holds as spend increases. If CPL climbs above ~$4.50, the cost-per-click shifts in favor of lead magnet.
  • Don't kill lead magnet entirely: It provides cost-efficient volume and reduces dependency on iOS-only audiences. If Apple further changes privacy policies, an all-iOS list carries platform risk.
  • Get downstream conversion data: Revenue-per-click by segment is the missing piece. If video subscribers also convert at a higher rate per click (not just more clicks), the case for video becomes even stronger.
  • Content optimization is the biggest lever: Regardless of segment, improving email content to push verified CTR higher across the board compounds massively over 365 sends. Even a 0.1% CTR improvement = significant annual click gains.
  • Consider the MPP discount: If video's real open rate is closer to 35-40% (not 50%), cost per click rises to ~$3.70–$4.20 vs. lead magnet's $2.94. Monitor this by tracking absolute click volumes, not percentages.
