## 1. Via FREE Facebook

This means you won't need to record a video. You just need to upload a **random photo** — or the photo that usually comes in the notes or cookie file when you purchase accounts.

## 2. Using Your Own Eyes via Smartphone Camera

Facebook's algorithms track all head turns **by the eyes**, not by the face itself (as you might logically assume). This means you can pass the selfie **without showing your full face**.

**How to do it:**
- Turn on your front-facing camera
- Film so that **only your eyes** are visible in the frame

The system won't be able to determine gender or age, but there's a high probability it will let you through. If you don't want to show your face (or a face generated by AI), you'll need to move on to other methods.

## 4. Using a Virtual Video

Generate a **fully virtual video** where a person makes the required head turns for you. You can specify all the turn timings directly in the prompt.

**Tools to generate the video:**

**Two options for using the video:**

| Verification Device | Method |

The key is that your avatar's head movements must be **synchronized** with Facebook's requirements.
