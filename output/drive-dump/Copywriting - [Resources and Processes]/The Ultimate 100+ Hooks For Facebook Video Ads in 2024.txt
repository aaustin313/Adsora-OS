* So what is a “hook”?

A hook is a part of the ad that stops your ideal customer from scrolling and makes them watch past the first 2-5 seconds of your ad.


It’s arguably the most important part of the ad because if your ad doesn’t attract the right audience, nothing else matters.




There are generally 6 types of hooks:
   1. Pattern Interrupt 
   2. Claim:
   1. In the next X seconds, I am going to show you how [I achieved benefit]
   3. Calling out to the audience:
   1. - who they are (if you’re a startup growth marketer)
   2. - their current circumstances (if iOS14 has decimated your Facebook ads tracking…)
   3. - their problems (if your ads are going on wild swings)
   4. - their desires (if you want to increase profit from ads)
   4. Disqualifying the audience and calling out the audience
   1. - if your campaigns have 5x ROAS, and you’re scaling like crazy and XYZ, you can go ahead and stop watching this video… however if you…
   5. Act Out The Problem OR Mistake Your Client/Customers Make
   1. If you’re doing [something] like this… you’re [making a giant mistake/wasting your ad dollars]
   6. A recent event or what’s happening in the industry that your audience can identify with
   1. XYZ happened… This means…




Hooks can also be categorized as:
   * Problem-Focused
   * Product-Focused
   * Benefit-Focused

Other hooks:
1. Breaking news!
 This hook creates a sense of urgency and importance, making it ideal for introducing new products or significant updates.
2. You'll never believe it
This hook sparks curiosity and is great for introducing surprising product benefits or unique features.
3. This will blow your mind
Similar to "You'll never believe it," this hook teases something extraordinary, perfect for innovative or groundbreaking products.
4. I promise you've never seen anything like this before
This hook suggests uniqueness and can be effective for products with new or unusual features.
5. You don't want to miss this!
It creates a fear of missing out (FOMO), useful for limited-time offers or exclusive deals.
6. This is an absolute gamechanger
Ideal for products that offer a new approach or significantly better solutions than existing ones.
7. Things that are worth every penny
This hook can be effective for premium products, emphasizing their value for money.
8. Quick reminder!
Great for retargeting campaigns or reminding customers of ongoing offers or benefits.
9. What if I told you that ____?
This hook is perfect for challenging common beliefs or introducing innovative concepts in your product.
10. POV: (insert a situation your ICP Could relate to)
11. I am about to (insert something shocking that happens at the end of the video)
            Example: I am about to drink toilet water
12. The rumors are…
13. This ____ hack feels illegal to know

   1. “I AM 61 POUNDS LIGHTER…” 
   2. Measure the speed of the claim 
   1. “FEEL BETTER FAST”
   2. “IN 2 SECONDS” 
   3. Compare the claim to it’s (unnamed) rival
   1. “SIX TIMES WHITER WASHES THAN THE LEADING COMPETITOR”
   4. Metaphorize the claim
   1. “MELTS AWAY UGLY FAT”
   5. Sensitize the claim by making the prospect feel, smell, touch, see or hear it 
   1. “THE SKIN YOU LOVE TO TOUCH” 
   6. Demonstrate the claim by showing a prime example
   1. “AT 60 MILES AN HOUR, THE LOUDEST NOISE IN THIS ROLLS ROYCE IS THE ELECTRIC CLOCK!” 
   7. Dramatize the claim, or its result 
   1. “THEY LAUGHED WHEN I SAT DOWN AT THE PIANO-BUT WHEN I STARTED TO PLAY…”
   8. Remove limitations from the claim 
   1. “SHRINKS HEMORRHOIDS WITHOUT SURGERY!”
