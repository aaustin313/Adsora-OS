The Funnel Architecture & Economics


The price is determined by input + output = Outcome / Price


Take 10 points, 1-10 and determine how much input they have to make to get the outcome.


Then take 10 more points and list out how much output the system / thing gets them.


If input is at 1 and output at 10, you can charge at the high end of the market.


If input is 10 and output is a 2, you have to charge less. 


The more they have to do, the less you can charge, and the more they get out of it.


So if on a scale of 1-10 on

Upsell 1…………..…..…….. $497-$997
Upsell 1 Downsell..……….. $297-$497
Upsell 2….………..……..…..$297-$397
Upsell 2 Downsell…………..$197-$297

The Story / Pitch Structure
  



Maximize / Minimize / Simplify


* Maximize (output) features, benefits and outcomes 


* Minimize (input) the required work effort and input


* Simplify the process


Use your natural selling process
* Your selling process should be a mirror image of your buying process
* Use it to your advantage


Notes:


* The interviewer has to be genuinely interested and curious about what you’re talking about
* He’s half of the sales team
* He will push along the sales process and energize the audience
* The thing you are selling is 50% of the deal, so make it sexy
* The other 50% is your story, so make that sexy too


Step 1 - Opening


* Begin with the end in mind
* This is the teaser of what they will get (the gift) at the end of the podcast


Step 2 - Introduction


* Introduce yourself and the podcast and the guest
* You are starting out introducing yourself and the guest
* The guest has come back from adventure and has returned with a story
* And also the gift from his heroes journey

Transition statements: “Welcome to the podcast name, today’s guest is (name) who has (list achievements) and today he’s going to be talking about / sharing (biggest benefit to the end user), we’re goig to be going into (list out the things)....


…Welcome to the podcast (name)


Step 3 - Start out in the ordinary world (where you were)


* This is the flip over into how it all began (remember, you teased them with the outcome and who you are)
* Were were they, what they were doing and WHO they were
* They’re unhappy in this world
* They know there is a better way

Transition statements: That’s pretty amazing, so what was it that got you pulled into this (what they’re doing)


Step 4 - External call to adventure & refusal / rejection of the call to adventure


   * Something happens that caused to the hero in the ordinary world that is a call to adventure
   * A call to adventure can mean losing weight, getting healthy, fighting off disease, getting rich, anything basically that lights a fire under their ass to get them to move
   * This call to adventure is short lived because it’s usually an idea and they don’t have a “how” so they reject / refuse the call
   * This is the state of fear they’re in


Transition statements: Wow (relate to it and build rapport, as you’re building rapport this is forcing the audience to build rapport with the story), so what happened next? 


Step 5 - Meeting with the mentor 


   * They meet the mentor who shows them the first step of the path that they need to take in order to go on their journey
   * They can’t see beyond this path and the hero thinks that that’s all there is to it
   * Once they complete this part of the journey, they’ll be done
   * Little do they know…


Transition statements: So that’s was it huh…this is how you got into this, ok awesome…so what happens next?


Step 6 - Crossing the threshold & Tests, allies and enemies


   * They have to cross the threshold and there is no looking back
   * This is the point of no return
   * After they cross the threshold
   * They enter a whole new world of challenges, tests, allies and enemies
   * This is where mistakes are made
   * Allies are formed
   * And enemies are made


Transition statements: This is the low point, so the energy has to lower and you have to really feel for the challenge they went thru and everything they’ve overcome.


Step 7 - Revelation (Death, abyss & rebirth) & Transformation


   * The hero goes through all of this and has to enter the innermost cave.
   * They have to defeat themselves and change
   * They change to become stronger than ever and transform in to defeat the external forces that are keeping them from getting the outcome they want


Transition statements: Relate to this and how this is the key to everything, this revelation, and the transformation to getting what they want


Step 8 - Atonement & The Prize


   * Once they’ve overcome their internal limitations and overcome external limitations
   * They now have the prize. It can be the body of their dreams, a million dollars, etc…


Transition statements: That’s the dream right…(repeat what they said)


Step 9 - Return With The Gift


   * They now return to the once ordinary world with new found strength, attitude, identity, skills, capabilities, stories and know hows where they’re sharing it with others


Step 10 - The “How Much” 


   * No one asks “How much” unless they’re ready to buy
   * Once the story concluded, the podcast host will ask “that’s pretty amazing, so how can others work with you to get this outcome?” or “that’s pretty amazing, what do you think the best way is for others to get the outcome”.
   * An outcome can be a “million dollars” in this case
   * And the response should be a choice
   * They can either go at it alone or they can (insert offer) and short cut all of the trials and tribulations
   * In a sense they can speed up their own heroes journey


Step 11- The Pitch


   * The podcast host then elegantly switches over and says “yeah lets talk about that for a moment”
   * So you have a program that does (insert what they do)
   * Then the answer is “oh yeah and we help so many people”
   * The podcast host asks, so what’s it usually cost?
   * The person being interviewed will say “Well you know, usually we charge X, but today…we’ll do 50% off for your guests if they buy within the next hour”
