Active vs Non-Active Symbolic Language


When you’re speaking or writing, you’re communicating 4 levels


1. Words
2. Images
3. Emotions / feelings
4. Symbolism 


As you’re doing this, you’re mapping and remapping your mind all the time.


* I want to make a $100m 
   * I want to = I don’t have yet
   * I want to = I lack it
   * I want to = I haven’t yet
      * On a symbolic level this communicates that you can’t do it, on a deeper level it communicates that you’re not going to do it
* I will make $100m
   * I will = I haven’t yet
   * I will = I can, but I haven’t decided yet
   * I will = I have the ability to, but I’m afraid to
      * On a symbolic level this communicates some level of LOSS, if you can will it, why aren’t you doing it?


* I can make a $100m
   * I can = but can you really? Why haven’t you?
   * I can = but can you really? How do you know?
   * I can = ok what evidence do you have?
      * On a symbolic level you say to yourself that you can, but because you haven’t, the subconscious mind knows you can’t.


* Other examples: I’m going to, etc…


One of the things you have to realize is that your subconscious mind is always in motion, it’s always active.


The reason we know that is because your heart continues to beat, you grow hair, you have energy cycles, hold blood pressure, etc…


Any type of language that’s OUTSIDE of MOTION is AGAINST the subconscious mind.


If it’s not in motion, it’s not real.


I am making $100m
I am gaining 7% bodyfat
I am building a business


The subconscious mind only believes what is true.


The creative process is ACTIVE

Symbolic Language Linking


Marketing is changework.


And by default in order to do marketing, we must communicate.


This is where copywriting comes in, it’s a communication tool.


Sales is also a communication tool.


So therefore all this falls under “messaging” 


What type of message we send out and what the message says, and what message is received.


* What it says
* What we send out
* What’s received (what it means)


There are four levels of communication:


Words
   1. Jr copywriters / marketers
Images
   2. Novice copywriters / marketers
Emotions
   3. Pro copywriters / marketers
Symbolism 
   4. Master copywriters / marketers


Word = dog
Image = image of a dog


A word WITHOUT an image is an abstraction.


What’s the #1 thing that kills copy? Abstraction.


That’s why “expensive” words don't work, because most people can’t form images from them.


Latin words and anglo-saxon words


A sentence WITHOUT an image is a bigger abstraction.


One other way we can decrease abstraction is by adding actual IMAGES to support the message.


When sentences become paragraphs they start to form EMOTIONS.


If sentences or paragraphs do not have emotion, they end up being descriptive or educational.


At the end, what did you really say?


The brain / mind can process ONLY so much information.


So it has to have an efficient way to “get the point across”


It’ll take words, images, emotions and it’ll form into symbolism.


You look at the whole thing and say what are you really saying?


Biggest brands in the world are symbols.


Symbols carry a lot of meaning.


I wanted to write the post about how people want to do more rather than learn.


Symbolic communication is telling them that they should NOT learn.


NHB+ / FF = learning


What people say and HOW they say it and their CHOICE of words carry a lot more meaning than you might be aware.


How many of you say “I work hard” or know someone who says “they work hard”?


What does the word HARD mean?


Words are nothing more than references to experience in the mind.


What’s your experience with hard?


What’s hard? Rocks hard, diamonds are hard, concrete is hard, surfaces are hard.


How can work be “hard”.


In Bosnian, working hard is “Tesko”.

Tesko je


Tesko means HEAVY


So I work “Tesko” = heavy


If we link language with SYMBOLS that represent something difficult then it’s difficult.


So saying “that’s a heavy task”, that hits harder than “it’s HARD”.


Hard is an abstraction


Heavy is an experience, you can connect language to experience which works A LOT better.


You have language that’s either rooted in ABSTRACTION or REALITY.


Words and sentences and paragraphs without images = abstraction.


Words and sentences and paragraphs that use terms without experience = abstraction.


Realty = something that we have experience in.


It’s HARD = abstraction


Heavy = reality.


You can say “it’s hard” but you don’t believe it cuz you can’t connect to it.

Each word has a word set of meaning, you have to be careful about using certain words.

This is useful in marketing because we can create better imagery.
This is also what allows from choice to no choice or no choice to choice.
Say for example you say to yourself “I’m confused” What do you do? You’re implying that you don’t know what to do.
Instead you can say “I’m unsure of these two things”.
* Past - this is BIG
* Former - in the past
* Previously - This happened
* One-Time - SMALLER
* This isn’t ALWAYS, these are SPECIFICS 
Knowing when to SHIFT the words to be more specific is key to everything.
When do you go from general language to specific language?
Specifics = details of the HOW
Specific if you want to move forward FROM something.
General, when you talk about your past and desires.
Generalized language in the past and to stay present, SPECIFIC to move forward.
So if we have VSL, TSL, or webinar, or anything else or AD…
We start GENERAL and then we  move into SPECIFICS down the path
If you start specific early on you overload them.
Specific to move forward, a specific word would move you forward.
In the beginning you use general language, as you go down you use more specific, specific language is the byproduct of HOW, and HOW helps them understand how something works.
At the end of everything what do they expect? What do they want? What are they there for?
They are there for an OUTCOME
What gets them the outcome? A process
Either they use the process
The product does the process for them
Or you do the process for them
There is always a process
The HOW is the word of the PROCESS
If they use the process, they need certainty of outcome otherwise they won’t buy, so what do they need to BELIEVE it? SPECIFICS of the HOW
If the product does the process, they don’t need to know the how, they just need to know that it works.
If you go through the process for them, they want to know what all you will do for them, so they don’t have to do it.
In either case, this is used to justify the trade offs, to calculate them.
Without a trade off calculation, making a decision becomes very difficult.
So a CTA anywhere (call to action) and our actualization of it depends on them making a decision.
But if they can’t do the math or the math isn’t in their favor, they won’t make the decision.
As we go from mid pitch to the end, we get more and more and more and more and more specific.
The perfect pitch
The 1st half shows them the words, images, and emotions and symbols
The 2nd half shows them the specifics of how all this will come true for them
We start general -> we end specific
To further the thought, what is an ad? It’s very general.
You’re pitching a generality, and selling a specificity. 
So the upper part of the page should be general and LIGHT, and then as you go down the page or DEEP into the VSL you can go deeper into the specifics.
