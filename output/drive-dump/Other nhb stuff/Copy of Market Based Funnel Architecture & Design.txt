Market-Based Funnel Architecture & Design






Principles 


* Every market is unique.
* And has a unique timeline of problems
* Think of funnels as timelines
* A funnel is nothing more than a series of decisions
* The more decisions they can make in fewer steps, the more money you’ll make
   * If you have 3 upsells, without downsells, it’ll do well = three decisions (you actually have 3 upsells)
      * Upsell 1 = 15%
      * Upsell 2 = 14%
      * Upsell 3 = 13% = $50 = $6.5 = $5 AOV contribuiton 
   * If you have 3 upsells and 3 downsells = 6 decisions (you actually have 6 upsells)
      * Upsell 1 = 15% = $197 = $29.55 AOV contribution
      * Upsell 1 downsell = 8% = $97 *.08 = $7.76 AOV contribute
         * All in of $37.31
      * Upsell 2 = 8% = $147 = $11.76 AOV contribution. 
         * 14% = $20.58 = $8.82 difference
      * Upsell 2 down-sell = 4%
      * Upsell 3 = 3% = $1.5 AOV contribution?
      * Upsell 3 downsell = 1%
   * If you have 3 upsells, with downsells but AFTER, it’ll do well = three decisions (you actually have 3 upsells)
      * Upsell 1 = 15%
      * Upsell 2 = 14%
      * Upsell 3 = 13% = $50 = $6.5 = $5 AOV contribuiton 
      * Upsell 1 - Downsell - 8%
      * Upsell 2 - Downsell - 5%
      * Upsell 3 - Downsell - 3% 
   * NO track stack
      *    * Upsell elasticity is 100x - for every $1 you get, you can charge $100 in the upsell path and it will convert.
      * If you double the price
      * Converting rate will go from 25% to about 18%
      * 28% reduction in CONVERSION, but 100% increase in price
      * You’ll have about 
* So you look at AOV contribution.
   * For example if your upsell 1 is $100, and CVR is 15% = $15 AOV contribution
* The bump will change the psychology of a buyer.
