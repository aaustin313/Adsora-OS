Mindset


How many of you tend to predict the future?


We can not function without predicting the future.


We can’t make ANY decisions, we can’t take any action, and we can’t do much of anything UNLESS we predict the future.


So the mind is a meaning-making machine.


But ultimately it is a PREDICTION ENGINE


So the reason we have memory is so we can predict.


Memory serves no other purpose in the mind.


Why do we need memory? So if you walk down the street, if you learn how to drive a car, if you do anything, then your ability to REDO IT and to get the SAME yet SAFE outcome AGAIN requires memory and predicting the outcome based on memory.


This is very useful but also the thing that tends to negatively affect us quite a bit.


Mind reading


This is where this prediction mechanism goes sideways.


Based on deletion, distortion and generalization fused with bias etc…


A lot of what you fear, what you are worried about, a lot of your anxieties, concerns, etc…


How you act, what limits you, what empowers you is based on mind reading.


Truth vs Trust, we use the memory thing to predict the future.


I’m going to do X, and if I do X, then Y will think of me _______________.


This also manifests as imposter syndrome, if I do X, then people will think of me as imposter.


Another way it predicts the future is…if I do X, then Y will happen.


Most of the days are the SAME because we just repeat memory cycles.


Habits are just strong memory cycles.


To stop mind reading is create a new rule in your thinking process where when you say something such as:


“If I X, Y will think of me”


Modify it and add 5-7 OTHER things they could think of you.


What else could they think? Write down as many as you can.


As you do that, what you’ll find is that your brain is just making shit up, because if something was going to happen, it would be ONE and ONLY, there would be NO other possibility.


If I do X, then Y will happen.


So you modify it and you go what else could happen?


List out EVERYTHING else that could happen, and what you will see once again is how your brain is mind reading and making shit up.


So one of the greatest things you can do to succeed is remap your memories.


Who you are, what you’re capable of, and how you’re different now, etc…


If you do not separate yourself from your memories, then you can not reinvent yourself.


What you do in the future will be within the safety bounds of the memory of the past, so what you can do is outline and figure out how you are different today than you were, how is the world diffnet, how are the capabilities different, etc…


And if it’s all different, which it was, then the memory can not hold true any more.


If you think about what you’re really afraid of, it’s often not found in your memory.

Engineering Commitment

In marketing what we’re doing is just getting people to make decisions.


Marketing is change work and until someone decides, nothing changes.


How is a decision measured?


Most other people will think that a decision is made when someone expresses it verbally.


This isn’t a decision.


I don’t care what someone says, because until they do it, they haven’t decided.


Behavior is the highest form of truth, it supersedes verbal communication. 


Pay attention to what they do, not what they say.


“When someone who’s you who they are, believe them.”


And the way they do that is by they behave, not what they say.


Me saying something doesn’t require a trade off, but me doing it does.


So all value is in behavior, and that’s why us as marketers are influencers and modifiers of human behavior.


In this concept of what a decision really is, people try to push for a DECISION in copy, but they are pushing for the wrong thing.


Decide to buy, decide to buy, decide to buy.


This is weak, because it’s removed from the precursor that needs to take place in order to make a decision via behavior, which is one of commitment.
 
Knowing how to get them to make a decision to commit is the 90% of the decision to BUY.


What people do is they focus on pushing the decision to BUY, without pushing the decision to COMMIT.


Once you know how to get them to commit, then the buying side ends up being very easy.


Unless a customer commits to buy from you, there’s always that exit door.


This exit door is exemplified by bounce rates, “let me think about it”, “i’ll come back later”, “i’ll do some more research” etc…


And just about everyone in direct response Fs this up because of their data.


50% bounce rates, 3-10% conversion rate, etc…


Very few people decide.


And those that do decide are the ones that committed.


When you’re running an offer, a lot of people take that exit door.
