* Say it differently 
   * One of the most powerful things you can do when you communicate with someone is be able to understand exactly what they see in their minds and for them to see what is in your mind.
      * This is the nature of effective communication
      * Anything that’s NOT seen is miscommunication
      * If you have any miscommunication in your messaging it tends to create resistance in your copy / marketing
      * Resistance
         * When you 1st start writing copy, you learn how everything works and then you start using additive copy
         * Simply means you put more words, more stuff on the page
         * With additive copy you just add add add, but then they stop reading and consuming
         * You gotta switch over to reductive optimization, which is knowing what to remove
         * Mastery level is knowing what NOT to say
      * You remove or reduce resistance and conversions go up
      * Same principle as putting a car in an air tunnel to reduce drag and increase the drag coefficient 
   * One of the things that creates resistance is how you say something
   * When I do HS / FF or answer questions, if someone says something, I’ll say “Say that differently” or “ask that differently”
      * On the 2nd or 3rd try, I can understand it much better
   * So the same thing applies to all forms of copy
   * All you have to do is say things differently
   * So if you have a headline, write 3 headlines, same thing but said differently
   * Same ad, but say it differently
   * Most people try too hard to come up with hookes, angles, story lines, etc…when sometimes they just need to say things differently

* Belif Validations


   * Once you do identify the beliefs and such, you look at the thoughts and then you can look at the way the thoughts express themselves.
   * The expression of thoughts will give you a clearer shape of the belief that is casting the shadow
   * In order for a belief to be a belief, it has to always be validated.
   * Every single belief you have is validated, always.
   * There is always some type of evidence that says this is true, otherwise you wouldn’t believe it
   * How do you know that it’s being validated?
      * Well you look at how it expresses itself in time
      * “I am __________” vs “I used to _________” vs “I am going to ____________”
      * I am _________ = this is actively validating
      * I used to ________ = this is something that you used to but not longer do and it has been replaced by I am ____________
      * I am going to _________ = this tells you the limitation otherwise you would have already done it or taken action on it
   * The negatives put them in the past = I used to ______________
      * It stops validating it as true
   * Take the future and put into the present
      * I am going to ________ to I am ___________
* When you do this, the belief is forced to validate itself, and as long you force it to validate itself in the RIGHT direction, it’ll give you what you want by becoming what you want it to be, then it’ll help your identity grow.

"Learning' vs. "doing" elements in a funnel and their effect on conversions


People buy for outcomes, not features or benefits. 


Features and benefits lead to outcomes.


So, whenever you have too many "learning elements" they are required to learn -> do -> get the outcome, so they are far removed in time from the outcome and thus have to expend much more energy.


If you add more DOING elements and DONE FOR YOU elements (templates, etc...) it'll move them much CLOSER to the OUTCOME and greatly increase the thruput of the funnel conversion rate.


Pay attention to how much they have to learn, do and have done for them in order to get the outcome they're seeking.

Btw it’s really interesting that having certain words in copy that imply LONG TERM thinking reduce the CVR by a lot


The rules are:


1. Pay attention to any language that has a timeline attached to it, be it months, years, etc…and that implies it will take that long to get the outcome.
2. Pay attention to any language that requires a long time to LEARN something and do it
3. Pay attention to any language that requires a LONG TERM commitment to something to get the outcome
   1. E.g. You have to hit the gym every day for 3 years.
4. This applies to yourself and to them, so if it takes YOU that long, it’ll take them EVEN LONGER
   1. They’ll always think it takes longer for THEM to get the outcome than it took you
5. Pay attention to anything that requires complexity
6. Basically anything with a LONG term timeline 
   1. If you get to 5 months, don’t say 5 months or month 1, month 2, month 3, etc…
   2. Say next month and month after that
7. Emphasize short term thinking UP FRONT in the copy


Short term thinking -> long term benefit
