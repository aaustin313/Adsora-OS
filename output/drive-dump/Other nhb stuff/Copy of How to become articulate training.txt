Overusing dead phrases:
Don't use phrases like time will tell etc
They are overused and dont stand out


Small surface lexicon:
This is the number of words (1k-1500 that you use and hear daily)




Thought retention:
You can't stay on one track, good speakers will peel apart the core meaning behind the argument or statement they’re trying to make which allows them to build up to it


Speech as a product of inputs:
You need to improve your inputs so track where you’re getting words from and what quality of words you’re hearing




The 3x5 Language Diet:
Do 5 minutes of each of the following daily


1.Reading material 1 level beyond your current average
language input 
Article or book chapter that uses more articulate words than you’re used to so you can expand your surface lexicon
Helps solve poor thought retention 




2. Reading poetry aloud to learn rhythm and
Cadence
Sentences sound better when words flow and fit into eloquent molds
Learn rhythm and cadence and tonality 
Your mouth has to feel how it feels to say eloquent phrases
Your changing your precut natural go to phrases rom the dead phrases into beautiful and original ones




3. Consciously attuned to the word choice of
an audio source


Knowledge is the information
Eloquence is how its delivered
Focus just on the eloquence and dont listen for the knowledge
Visualize in your mind the words being said






Increasing consciousness per sentence
