Most people in the game are focused on just generating customers.


The ONLY constant that holds true is that the cost of ads continue to go up.


If you look at any type of economic breakthrough in DR / Marketing it’s a new tech platform with cheap ads.


Facebook - 2014 cheap ads
Google 2004 - 2 cents a click
TikTok - cheap ads
Etc…


So long as you have cheap ads, you can make good money, pretty easily.


If everyone has access to the same advantage, soon enough that advantage will become a disadvantage.


If the cost of ads goes up, which it will, there’s only one thing to do, which is scale in the negative.


If ads go up, you have to lose money to make money.


Then the game becomes about whoever is BEST at losing money is going to make the most money.


It becomes all about “deep back ends” and repeat customers.


It’s all going to become repeat business.


Those who have repeat business = win, those who do not = lose.


Those that do this 1st, take position in the  market to own the market.


Warren Buffet said:


“I couldn't pay you $10k NOT to use an iphone”


So if someone can pay your customers NOT to do business with you, then you have zero moat.


Because we operate in OPEN MARKETS and as such anyone, anywhere can run and POACH your customers. 


But when they run ads, the customer has to pay THEM, so if they can’t pay your customers to leave, you never have to think about it.


This brings the risk of the business to be virtually zero.


At some point you have to get good at product, otherwise without product you have no business.


So in a sense, you’re not paying to acquire the customer.


You are paying to acquire the revenue.
