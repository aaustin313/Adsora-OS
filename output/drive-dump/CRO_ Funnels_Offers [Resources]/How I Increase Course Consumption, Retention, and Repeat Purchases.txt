How I Increase Course Consumption, Retention, and Repeat Purchases
A lot of creators get stuck on how to get course purchasers to actually use the thing they just bought.


Solving this issue is KEY to retention and maximizing LTV - especially when using something like the Catalog Model.


So what do people really want once they've purchased? It's actually pretty simple...


They want it to easily fit into their already busy life


^ they want it spelled out as to HOW to make that happen


They want to follow an active example so they feel like they're doing it WITH others vs following directions on their own


Here's my secret sauce to make it happen


I spell out doing certain tasks and implementing things as a short daily schedule for the week and send it to them in email.


In that email, I give them a printable task list as well as giving them those tasks setup digitally in Trello / Todoist / Notion / Clickup so they can duplicate the lists to their favorite interface.


I give them small tip snippets for each task one that I know they'll see in the course material and videos, so it functions as a "copy payoff"


I regularly demonstrate my own doing the tasks on an active project in the program community.


When you shift your mentality to one where "you're in a dating relationship" with your space, things like this start to appear as easy wins because you're focused on how to make their relationship with you the best one of their lives....


...and when you give people that, you get THEM to continually invest in you because the relationship dynamic is one they won't get anywhere else...


...And THAT is the real key to overcoming the limitation of time and attention for consuming anything you put into your space.
